\section*{Instructor Key}

\subsection*{Worksheet A}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Density increases with decreasing $\phi$.
  \item Fluid substitution effect: air $\ll$ water $<$ brine.
  \item Typical differences: $\sim$200--400 kg m$^{-3}$ between air- and water-filled.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Worksheet B}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Buried sphere: symmetric positive/negative bump; $\Delta\rho$ 200--500 kg/m$^3$.
  \item Cylinder/dike: narrow peak; width $\approx$ depth.
  \item Faulted contact: asymmetric shoulder.
  \item Basin: broad low-amplitude anomaly.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Worksheet C}
\[
  g_z(0) = \frac{4\pi G}{3}\Delta\rho\, a^3 \frac{1}{z_0^2}.
\]
Half-width:
\[
  \left(\frac{x_{1/2}}{z_0}\right)^2 + 1 = 2^{2/3}
\Rightarrow
  \frac{x_{1/2}}{z_0} = \sqrt{2^{2/3}-1}.
\]
Non-uniqueness: shallow lens can match peak amplitude and width if appropriately shaped.
