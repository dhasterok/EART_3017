\section*{Activity 3: Buried Sphere Width--Depth}

Given:
\[
  g_z(x) = \frac{4\pi G}{3}\Delta\rho\, a^3
          \frac{z_c}{(x^2+z_c^2)^{3/2}}.
\]

\subsection*{Tasks}
\begin{enumerate}
  \item Show that $g_z(0)$ is proportional to $\Delta\rho\,a^3/z_c^2$.
  \item Solve for $x_{1/2}$ such that $g_z(x_{1/2}) = \frac{1}{2}g_z(0)$.
  \item Derive:
  \[
    z_c \approx 0.65 w
  \]
  where \(w\) is the full-width half-maximum.  Hint: this is on the right track,
  \[
    \frac{z_c}{x_{1/2}} = (2^{2/3} - 1)^{-1/2}.
  \]
  \item Explain:  
        \begin{itemize}
          \item Why depth affects width but density contrast does not.  
          \item How the radius and density contrast result create non-uniqueness for inversion and how we ensure uniqueness.
          \item Why a shallow lenticular body could mimic a deep sphere's anomaly.
        \end{itemize}
\end{enumerate}
