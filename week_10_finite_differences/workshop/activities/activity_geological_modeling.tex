\begin{center}
\Large \textbf{Activity 10.5: Building Geological Intuition with Numerical Experiments} \\[0.5em]
\normalsize Geophysics — Numerical Heat Flow
\end{center}

\vspace{1em}

\section*{Purpose}
To use numerical experiments to explore how thermal structure responds to
geological complexity.

\section*{Thought Experiments}
Consider modifying a numerical heat flow model to include:
\begin{itemize}
  \item depth-dependent thermal conductivity,
  \item layered heat production,
  \item time-varying basal heat flux,
  \item transient magma intrusions.
\end{itemize}

\section*{Tasks}
\begin{enumerate}
  \item For each modification, predict qualitatively how the temperature field
  would respond.

  \item Identify which changes could still be modeled analytically and which
  require numerical approaches.

  \item Discuss how numerical models allow separation of physical effects that
  are difficult to isolate observationally.
\end{enumerate}

\section*{Key Insight}
Numerical modeling enables controlled experiments on Earth systems that cannot
be performed in nature.
